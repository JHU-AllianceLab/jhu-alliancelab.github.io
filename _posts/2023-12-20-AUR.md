title: "[IJRR'23] Active Uncertainty Reduction for Safe and Efficient Interaction Planning"

# Short description used for previews 
description: "To systematically reconcile safety and performance in designing human-centered autonomy, our key idea is to equip a safety filter with a task policy that predicts a wide range of possible interactions, induced by factors such as the human’s goal or alertness, between the robot and nearby humans. These multi-modal predictions enable the robot to preempt future costly safety filter interventions and, where possible, adjust its course of action to avoid the risk of having to apply an inefficient last-minute maneuver. This approach transforms conventional probabilistic safety (i.e., gambits) into prediction-centric performance tuning under strict safety guarantees."
